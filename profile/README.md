## What Kind of DDoS Attack Are You Actually Worried About?

Before jumping into solutions, it's worth knowing what you're up against. GTHost's own documentation breaks DDoS attacks into three main categories:

**Volume-based attacks** flood your connection with raw traffic — the goal is simply overwhelming your bandwidth pipe until nothing else gets through. These are the most common and the most dramatic.

**Network-layer (protocol) attacks** use malformed packets to exhaust server-side resources like firewall state tables and load balancers. You can have plenty of bandwidth left and still go down.

**Application-layer attacks** are subtler — they mimic legitimate user requests to exhaust server CPU and RAM. Harder to detect, harder to block without smart filtering.

The uncomfortable truth is that a dedicated server, no matter how powerful, is vulnerable to all three. As GTHost's blog points out: if someone can send a request to your server, they can theoretically DDoS it — it's purely a question of scale. What actually matters is whether your hosting provider has the upstream infrastructure to absorb or filter that traffic before it reaches your machine.

That's where the choice of hosting provider becomes your first and most important line of defense.
